Eating Disorders : Eating disorders are so common in America that 1 or 2 out every 100 students will struggle with one. The most common types of eating disorder are anorexia nervosa and bulimia nervosa (usually called simply “anorexia” and “bulimia”: But other food-related disorders, like binge eating disorders, body image disorders, and food phobias, are showing up more frequently than they used to.

ANOREXIA : People with anorexia have an extreme fear of weight gain and a distorted view of their body size and shape. As a result, they can’t maintain a normal body weight. Some people with anorexia restrict their food intake by dieting, or excessive exercise. They hardly eat at all – and the small amount of food they do eat becomes an obsession. Other people with eating disorders do something called binge eating and purging, where they eat a lot of food and then try to get rid of the calories by forcing themselves to vomit, using laxatives, or exercising excessively.

BULIMIA : Bulimia is similar to anorexia. With bulimia, a person binge eats (eats far too much food) and then tries to compensate it extreme ways, such as forced vomiting or excessive exercise, to prevent weight gain. Over time, these steps can be dangerous. To be diagnosed with bulimia, a person must be binging and purging regularly, at least twice a week for a couple of months. People with bulimia eat a large amount of food (often junk food) at once, usually in secret. The person typically feels powerless to stop eating and can only stop once he or she is too full to eat any more.

1. Síntomas de la bulimia


Es habitual que la persona bulímica tenga un peso totalmente normal, pero que se vean a sí mismas con sobrepeso. Así, los signos más habituales de la bulimia son:

* Repetir episodios de ingerir cantidades excesivas de alimentos en una única vez

* Durante el atracón, la persona tendrá una pérdida de control, sin poder dejar de comer o sin ser capaz de controlarlo

* Tras un atracón, forzar el vómito

* Hacer ejercicio excesivo para no aumentar el peso

* Utilizar laxantes, diuréticos después de comer

* Ayunar

* La gravedad de la bulimia se determina en función de las veces por semana en la que se producen atracones y su extensión en el tiempo.

2.como podemos decir que una persona sufre de bulimia

Una persona puede sufrir de bulimia cuando presenta síntomas como vómitos recurrentes después de comer, uso excesivo de laxantes o diuréticos, y preocupación obsesiva por el peso y la apariencia física. La bulimia es un trastorno alimenticio que puede tener serias consecuencias para la salud física y mental de la persona que lo padece. Si sospechas que alguien cercano a ti podría estar sufriendo de bulimia, es importante que lo apoyes y lo animes a buscar ayuda profesional.

3. por que los bulimicos comen tanto ?

Las personas con bulimia a menudo comen en exceso debido a una combinación de factores psicológicos y fisiológicos. Pueden sentir una necesidad compulsiva de comer, seguida de sentimientos de culpa y vergüenza que los llevan a provocarse el vómito. Este ciclo se repite debido a un desequilibrio en los neurotransmisores y hormonas que regulan el apetito y el estado de ánimo. Es importante que las personas con bulimia reciban apoyo y tratamiento profesional para romper este patrón dañino.

Repeat episodes of eating excessive amounts of food at one time
During the binge, the person will have a loss of control, unable to stop eating or being unable to control it
After a binge, force vomiting
Exercising excessively to avoid gaining weight
Use laxatives, diuretics after eating

2.when can we say that a person suffers from bulimia ?

A person may suffer from bulimia when they have symptoms such as recurrent vomiting after eating, excessive use of laxatives or diuretics, and obsessive concern about weight and physical appearance. Bulimia is an eating disorder that can have serious consequences for the physical and mental health of the person who suffers from it. If you suspect that someone close to you might be suffering from bulimia, it is important that you support them and encourage them to seek professional help.

3.Why do bulimics eat so much?

People with bulimia often overeat due to a combination of psychological and physiological factors. They may feel a compulsive need to eat, followed by feelings of guilt and shame that lead them to make themselves vomit. This cycle repeats itself due to an imbalance in the neurotransmitters and hormones that regulate appetite and mood. It is important that people with bulimia receive professional support and treatment to break this harmful pattern.
